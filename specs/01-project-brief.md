# Save the Novel — Project Brief

## Project Name
**Save the Novel** — *The Reader Speaks*

## Tagline
*"Let the reader speak!"*

## Manifesto

> We the Novel Readers of the World, in Order to form a more healthy literary landscape, establish connection, insure creative ecology, give voice to the common reader, promote the aspiring novelist, and secure the Blessings of Literature to ourselves and our Posterity, do ordain and establish SAVE THE NOVEL.
>
> **The Reader Speaks.**

## The Problem

In the United States, educators must optimize for what can be captured on a test. As a result, "informational texts" are prioritized over novels. Literature is presented in the form of excerpts and passages — preparation for standardized assessments, not meaningful engagement with a text.

A whole generation has been educated to read for comprehension-only, without meaningful connection to what they read.

Now that AI can digest and summarize instantly, what reason does that generation have to pick up a novel at all?

## The Insight

The real value of literature lies in what cannot be quantified:

- **Empathy** — inhabiting lives and perspectives beyond your own
- **Tolerance for ambiguity** — sitting with questions that have no clean answers
- **Holding contradictory ideas simultaneously** — the kind of cognitive complexity that builds real wisdom
- **Connection and belonging** — the deeply personal experience of being changed by a story

Readers know this. But if we can't *demonstrate* why we read novels — if we can't make a compelling case for long-form reading — then how can we ensure the future preservation of the craft?

## The Solution

A website that gathers and broadcasts the **experience of reading** — not for information, but for connection, belonging, empathy, nuance.

The core content is a collection of **personal essays / letters to the authors** of the literature that changed readers' lives. Each essay answers the questions:

- Where were you when you read the novel that changed you?
- Who were you with?
- Who were you?
- How were you changed by the experience of reading it?

## Origin Story

Several bookstores in the Southwest have agreed to let S.E. Elkins host events where local authors share the story of the novel that changed their lives. These personal stories of novel-reading serve as a call to action:

- To read more
- To read deeply
- To support artists and bookstores
- To remember that storytelling has always been essential to our humanity

Each published essay will be linked to the contributor's favorite independent bookstore — reinforcing the ecosystem that sustains literature.

## Mission Statement

To give readers a voice to promote the literature that changed their lives, to collect the stories of reading, to broadcast the rich blessing of literature.
